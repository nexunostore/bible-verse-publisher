Study Notes for John 1:1-10


The Gospel of Light

As we delve into the opening chapters of the gospel of John and Mark, we are introduced to two pivotal figures in the life of 
Jesus Christ: the authors of 1-John and John the Baptist.

The Authors' Personal Experience with Jesus Christ

In 1-John 1:1-2, the authors describe their personal experience with Jesus Christ. They claim to have seen and handled the Word of life,
which was with the Father and manifested unto them. This eyewitness account is a testament to the authenticity of their message.

The Message of God's Light

In 1-John 1:5-6, we learn that God is light and has no darkness at all. The authors emphasize the importance of walking in the light, as 
Jesus Christ is in the light. This theme is echoed in Mark 1:3, where John the Baptist prepares the way for Jesus by preaching repentance.

John the Baptist's Role

Mark 1:4-8 describes John the Baptist's role in preparing the way for Jesus. He baptized people in the Jordan River and preached 
repentance, acknowledging that he was not worthy to loosen the latchet of Jesus' shoes (Mark 1:7). This humility is a stark contrast to 
the pride and arrogance often seen in religious leaders.

Jesus' Baptism

In Mark 1:9-11, we witness Jesus' baptism by John. As Jesus emerged from the water, he saw the heavens open and the Spirit descend upon 
him. This event marked the beginning of Jesus' public ministry, where he would preach the gospel of God and call people to repentance.

The Call to Follow

Mark 1:12-20 describes Jesus' temptation in the wilderness and his subsequent ministry in Galilee. He preached the gospel of God, 
calling people to repentance and faith. Among those called was Simon, Andrew, James, and John, who left their nets and followed Jesus 
(Mark 1:17-20).

Conclusion

As we reflect on these opening chapters, we are reminded that the gospel is a message of light, hope, and redemption. The authors of 
1-John and Mark invite us to walk in the light, confess our sins, and be cleansed from unrighteousness (1-John 1:6-9). May we heed this 
call and follow Jesus Christ, who is the Son of God and the Light of the world.

* The authors' personal experience with Jesus Christ, having seen and handled the Word of life (1-John 1:1-2).
* Their purpose in writing, which is to declare their fellowship with God and Jesus Christ, and to have fellowship with the readers 
(1-John 1:3-4).
* The message that God is light and has no darkness at all (1-John 1:5).
* The importance of walking in the light and confessing sins to be cleansed from unrighteousness (1-John 1:6-9).

The gospel of Mark verses 1:1-21 describe,

* Jesus Christ as the Son of God, who came to preach the gospel of God (Mark 1:1).
* John the Baptist's role in preparing the way for Jesus by baptizing people in the Jordan River and preaching repentance (Mark 1:4-8).
* Jesus' baptism by John, where he saw the heavens open and the Spirit descend upon him (Mark 1:9-11).
* Jesus' temptation in the wilderness and his subsequent ministry in Galilee, where he preached the gospel of God and called Simon, 
Andrew, James, and John to follow him (Mark 1:12-20).
